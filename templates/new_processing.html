Histogram Equalization

Histogram equalization modifies the dynamic range of an image by altering the pixel values, guided by the intensity histogram of that image. Recall that the intensity histogram of an image depicts the number of pixels for each brightness value in the image. Histogram equalization operation enhances the contrast of image.

Discrete Cosine Transform

The discrete cosine transform (DCT) is not a multiscale transform, but its relevance to the topic of image separation discussed later justifies its brief description. The DCT is a variant of the discrete Fourier transform (DFT), known to be well suited for stationary signals obeying by first-order Markov models with high correlation.

Morphological Transformation

Morphological transformations are some simple operations based on the image shape. It is normally performed on binary images. It needs two inputs, one is our original image, second one is called structuring element or kernel which decides the nature of operation. Four basic morphological operators are Erosion, Dilation, Opening, and Closing.
